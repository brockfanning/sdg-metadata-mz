    Ministry of the Interior, administrative records

    Whenever there are cases of human trafficking, administrative data are collected manually through records of occurrences made daily in all police units, such as the Department for Assistance to Families, Minor Victims of Violence, Police Posts, Police Stations, Provincial Police Commands of the Republic of Mozambique (CPPRM) and Criminal Investigation Services (SERNIC)]

    Subsequently, they are sent to the General Command of the Police of the Republic of Mozambique (CGPRM) and this, in turn, sends them to the Directorate of Planning, Studies and Documentation for globalization for statistical purposes

    Until March 2021

    May 2021

    Ministry of Interior (MINT), through the National Criminal Investigation Service (SERNIC) and General Command of the Police of the Republic of Mozambique (CGPRM)

    Ministry of the Interior (MINT) - Directorate of Planning, Studies and Documentation, Department of Statistics

    By Resolution number 8/2018, of March 21, article 15, paragraph e) that approves the Organic Statute of the Ministry of Interior (MINT), which, among other duties of the Planning, Study and Documentation Directorate, directs and controls the process of collecting, processing, analyzing and inferring statistical information.

    According to the occurrence records, the calculation of this indicator measures the level of prevalence of victims of trafficking, and forms of exploitation.

    The number of victims of trafficking identified allows referring to victims, as defined in the UN Protocol, where the act, average and purpose of trafficking were identified by national authorities.

    While data on identified victims can provide valuable information for monitoring the sex and age profile of victims, as well as on forms of exploitation and trafficking flows, the number of victims identified alone does not monitor the level of human trafficking. Therefore, trends should be interpreted with caution, as changes in identified victims of trafficking may be due to several factors, such as the intensity of trafficking flows, but also changes in law enforcement practices, changes in legislation or victims' attitudes.

    The indicator is calculated using the ratio between the total number of victims of trafficking in persons discovered or living in a country and the population residing in the country, expressed per 100,000 inhabitants

    After the globalization of data and calculation of the indicator by the Statistics Department, it is submitted to a process of assessment and validation by the Director of Planning, Studies and Documentation of the Ministry of Interior

    There is no elaborated quality management system that makes it possible to assess data quality

    The verification of the data consistency that is made is in terms of calculation, if there is any inconsistency, the data review is requested to the source

    No quality assessment has yet been carried out, taking into account the standard quality criteria established by the United Nations.

    Data are made available annually and are not disaggregated.

    This indicator is internationally comparable.

    The periodic reports and balance sheets, through which the Ministry of the Interior monitors and evaluates institutional performance, are the only references
